{% raw %}What does “look forward to” mean in English?<br /><br />It means to think about something in the future with pleasure. To be pleased or excited that something is going to happen.<br /><br />The form and grammar rules are as follows.<br /><br />look forward to + noun phrase<br />Example: &quot;I'm looking forward to the weekend.&quot;<br /><br /><br />look forward to + ing form (gerund)<br />Example: Mark is looking forward to buying a house.<br /><br />We do NOT use the infinitive / base form of the verb:<br />&quot;I'm looking forward to meet you.&quot; (wrong)<br /><br />If the second verb has a different subject, we use the object pronoun.<br />Example: &quot;I'm looking forward to HIM visiting me next week.&quot;<br />We do NOT use the subject pronoun:<br /><br /><br />&quot;look forward to&quot; is very common in written English. We often use it at the end of a letter or email. It's a polite way of telling someone to do something.
