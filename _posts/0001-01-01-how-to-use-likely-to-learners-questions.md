{% raw %}Improve your English by learning how to use the phrase 'Be likely to' in this free tutorial from BBC Learning English.<br /><br />Watch the video, then try the quiz: <a rel="nofollow" target="blank" href="http://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/course/english-you-need/unit-8/session-5">http://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/course/english-you-need/unit-8/session-5</a> <br /><br />SUMMARY:<br /><br />Be likely to<br />We use be likely to to talk about the possibility of something happening, often in connection with the future.<br />When we say be likely to, we are confident that something will happen, but we cannot be 100% sure.<br />The American is likely to win the race tomorrow.<br />It’s likely to rain on Tuesday.<br /><br />Be unlikely to<br />The opposite of likely is unlikely. The opposite of be likely to is be unlikely to. This means we are confident that something will not happen.<br />England are unlikely to win the world cup.<br />He’s unlikely to pass his exams.<br /><br />Past<br />Be likely to and be unlikely to can be used in the past. Change the verb be. We usually use a perfect form after.<br />Sarah was unlikely to have known that her boyfriend was a criminal when she met him.<br />My father was unlikely to have lost his wallet in his own house.<br /><br />Do you want to learn how to speak English?
