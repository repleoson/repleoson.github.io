Scientific studies say that the air that you breathe indoors contains thousands of microscopic particles, such as dust, bacteria, viruses, cleaning chemicals, building materials, cooking byproducts, and pesticides.

These pollutants enter your respiratory system, harming the sensitive tissue in your lungs. Long-term exposure to these pollutants have led to increased asthma symptoms, productivity loss, severe illness, and even death.

The pandemic has made us realize how much the air around us can affect our health and that we must take utmost care to keep it clean and healthy. The best way to improve indoor air quality is to add air purifiers, which helps increase the amount of air changes inside, and scrubs the air.
