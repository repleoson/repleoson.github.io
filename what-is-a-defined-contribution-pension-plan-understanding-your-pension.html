Defined contribution pension plans are based on an employee contributing either a fixed amount or percentage of their earnings, with their employer often matching the employee‚Äôs contribution, to fund their retirement. As such, the contribution formula is defined, yet the pay out is not known. Instead, it is dependent on the success of the investment, with no guarantees. It tends to be voluntary and self-directed. It also has the advantage of investing on a tax-deferred basis, with withdrawals expected to occur when the employee, now a retiree, is in a lower tax bracket.
