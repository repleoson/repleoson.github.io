The UN Security Council has for the first time called for an end to the fighting in Ethiopia, but a meeting to discuss the crisis has been postponed until next week.

Ethiopia’s year-long conflict with rebels from the northern Tigray region has worsened recently with the Tigrayan People’s Liberation Front (TPLF) forces seizing key cities on a highway that leads towards the capital.

The Oromo Liberation Army and seven other groups from around the country have now joined the TPLF in the fighting.

Al Jazeera’s Mike Hanna reports from the United Nations, New York, the US.
