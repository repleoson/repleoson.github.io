Gerard Piqué, the Barcelona star defender is loved by his fans all over the world. His game has provided many people with endless moments of joy and happiness in their lives, whether it be the World Cup with Spain or the Champions League title win with Barcelona, his presence on the field has made him an important part of the footballing history.

But off the field, there are certain things that you probably didn’t know about Piqué:-

He is the grandson of Amador Bernabeu, who is a former vice-president of Barcelona.
Gerard had a good upbringing in his childhood, his father is a Businessman in Spain and his mother is the director of a spinal injuries hospital.
He went into a coma for two days when he was one and a half years old, he got injured while chasing the ball but fortunately gained Consciousness.
His idol was Fernando Hierro, who was surprisingly a Real Madrid legend.
He has a business degree focusing on sports, media, and entertainment from the prestigious Harvard Business School.
He met his future wife, Shakira while filming for the 2010 World Cup song in South Africa.
His favorite music artists are U2, Lady Gaga, and Rihanna.
He has many business interests, some of them are Kosmos holdings, that he opened with Hiroshi Mikitani, the founder of Rakuten.
He also has stakes in two Spanish football teams, FC Andorra and Gimnàstic Manresa.

Pique is one of three players to win successive Champions League with different teams

Today he’s an icon at Barcelona, his childhood club. But it’s easy to forget that he hasn’t spent his entire career at the Camp Nou. After coming through the ranks at the club’s famed La Masia academy he joined Manchester United in 2004 aged just 17.

There he won the Champions League in 2008 before moving back to Barcelona that summer and winning it again in 2009, becoming only the third player in history to do so after Marcel Desailly and Paulo Sousa. Since then he’s added two more Champions League titles (2011, 2015), taking his tally to four.
