Singapore is experiencing its worst pandemic wave since the start of the pandemic, reporting around 2000-3000 cases a day. And thats because it abandoned the "zero-covid" strategy, saying its not feasible in a long-term. To support its new "living with COVID" strategy, Singapore needs to get more people vaccinated and reduce the strain on the healthcare system. Around 90 percent of Singaporeans are fully vaccinated - but that number has to get even higher to reduce the strain on the healthcare system. Thats why its making life potentially more expensive for those choose not to be vaccinated
