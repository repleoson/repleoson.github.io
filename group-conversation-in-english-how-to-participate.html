If you’re an advanced or fluent English student you know that English conversation moves fast…especially when you’re talking with a group of people! I’ve found that helping students develop their group conversation skills builds students’ confidence and can help you feel certain that you know how to participate in those kinds of conversations. If you’re a non-native English speaker I’m guessing you’ve had the experience of feeling on the fringes of a group conversation that’s taking place in English. It’s a common complaint among my students. I’ve developed these 5 specific tips to help you feel more confident in your English conversation skills and bold enough to jump into group conversations. You’ll learn specific phrases you can use to participate in group conversation, how to use each skill and ways to be more participatory. If talking in a group has been frustrating, these 5 tips will help unlock your confidence and show you exactly how to have the kinds of conversations you want to have. Yes, English conversation moves rapidly, but there are specific things that you can do to help yourself! I’m excited to show you.
