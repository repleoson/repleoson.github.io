The palmar grasp reflex is elicited by applying gentle pressure onto or by horizontally stroking the infant‚Äôs palm. In response, the infant will close the palm or make a fist. Unilateral absence of the palmar grasp indicates a brachial plexus or peripheral nerve injury. Bilateral absence of the palmar and/or plantar grasp reflex is suggestive of cerebral palsy.
