Small Intestine Bacterial Overgrowth (SIBO) is a common cause of frequent burping, abdominal distention, pain and IBS symptoms. The good news is that it is something that can be diagnosed and resolved with treatment. This video covers the basics of symptoms, risk factors, diagnosis and treatment.

SIBO - What is it?
SMALL INTESTINE BACTERIAL OVERGROWTH â€“ abnormal overgrowth of bacteria in the upper intestine.

COMMON SYMPTOMS:
Abdominal distention, pain, bloating, gas, flatulence
Changes in transit time
Diarrhea
Constipation
Symptoms tend to be worse as the day progresses

SOME CAUSES/RISK FACTORS:
A history of food poisoning
Exposure to GI infection while traveling
Reduced hydrochloric acid production and proton pump inhibitors
Chronic stress
Neurologic changes that affect SI motility
Decreased motility of the small intestine due to structural issues like adhesions
